== Inline text formatting

Plain text can be made *bold*, _italic_, *_bold italic_*, `monospaced`,
`*bold monospaced*`, and `_italic monospaced_`.

You can also write ^superscript^ and ~subscript~, mark text with #highlight#,
and apply a role like [.underline]#underlined# or [.line-through]#struck through#.

Smart quotes turn "`double quoted`" and '`single quoted`' text into curly quotes,
and an apostrophe becomes typographic, as in Sarah's book.

Constrained vs unconstrained: the letters b**ol**d and i__tal__ic inside a word
require the doubled markup.

Special characters are escaped automatically: 1 < 2, 3 > 2, AT&T, and a literal
asterisk \*not bold*.

[.lead]
This paragraph uses the `lead` role, so it should appear larger than the body text.

Paragraphs are separated by a blank line.
A single newline inside a paragraph is rendered as a space, so these two lines
become one flowing sentence.

To force a line break, +
add a trailing plus sign. +
Like this.

[%hardbreaks]
Or set the hardbreaks option on the block.
Every newline is then preserved.
No plus signs required.
